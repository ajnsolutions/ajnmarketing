# Trust Model & Management Styles

**Companion to:** [`MAGIC_BLUEPRINT.md`](./MAGIC_BLUEPRINT.md) · [`VOICE_AND_PERSONALITY.md`](./VOICE_AND_PERSONALITY.md)

Trust is earned. Autonomy is bounded. Publishing and schedules remain explicitly gated until product + ops deliberately expand them.

---

## 1. Relationship stages

### 1.1 Assistant
- **Communication:** Highly explanatory; short lessons; “here’s why I’m asking”  
- **Approval expectations:** Nearly everything reviewed by owner  
- **Autonomy:** Draft and recommend only  
- **Owner involvement:** High (setup + frequent approvals)  
- **Success criteria:** Completes onboarding; understands approval loop  
- **Promotion criteria:** Completes setup; ≥N successful approvals without major edits/rejects; owner chooses Weekly Manager or similar  
- **Fallback:** If confusion or rejects spike → stay / step back; more explanation  

### 1.2 Coordinator
- **Communication:** Steady weekly cadence; plain English  
- **Approval expectations:** Weekly package primary path  
- **Autonomy:** Prepare full weekly set; wait for approval  
- **Owner involvement:** Weekly minutes  
- **Success criteria:** Habitual weekly review; low anxiety  
- **Promotion criteria:** Consistent approvals; low edit intensity; stable health  
- **Fallback:** Missed weeks → gentle reminders, not guilt  

### 1.3 Marketing Manager
- **Communication:** Crisp recommendations; less teaching  
- **Approval expectations:** Batch approvals; exceptions called out  
- **Autonomy:** Stronger sequencing of work; still no silent publish beyond policy  
- **Owner involvement:** Focused decisions  
- **Success criteria:** Owner trusts prioritization  
- **Promotion criteria:** High approval rate; management style allows more autonomy  
- **Fallback:** Controversial drafts → force Hands-On for that item  

### 1.4 Head of Marketing
- **Communication:** Confident, brief; “I handled X; need you on Y”  
- **Approval expectations:** Exception-based per management style  
- **Autonomy:** Highest allowed by style + product gates  
- **Owner involvement:** Exceptions + monthly outcomes  
- **Success criteria:** Owner feels marketing is handled  
- **Promotion criteria:** *(Future)* Executive Partner when strategy advisory is productized  
- **Fallback:** Any publish/incident failure → temporary Coordinator behavior  

### 1.5 Executive Partner *(future)*
- **Communication:** Business advisory tone  
- **Approval expectations:** Rare; strategy checkpoints  
- **Autonomy:** Within strict policy envelopes  
- **Owner involvement:** Goals and constraints  
- **Success criteria:** Business outcomes + retained trust  
- **Promotion criteria:** N/A (top of ladder for now)  
- **Fallback:** Revert to Head of Marketing on uncertainty

## 2. Management styles

Management style is the **owner’s preferred operating mode**. It replaces talking primarily about “approval frequency” as a naked setting.

### 2.1 Hands-On Owner
- **Ideal customer:** Risk-averse, new to AJN, regulated categories, perfectionists  
- **Communication frequency:** Per-item or dense weekly  
- **Automation level:** Low  
- **Approval behavior:** Approve/edit/reject each material change  
- **Exceptions:** Always surface legal/brand-sensitive drafts  
- **Recommended use:** First 30–90 days; law/medical/finance-adjacent  
- **Future expansion:** Per-channel hands-on toggles  

### 2.2 Weekly Manager *(default aspirational for most local owners)*
- **Ideal customer:** Busy trades and local services  
- **Communication frequency:** One weekly package (+ rare alerts)  
- **Automation level:** Medium  
- **Approval behavior:** Approve package or individual items; then queue/publish per product rules  
- **Exceptions:** Negative reviews, outages, policy flags  
- **Recommended use:** Steady-state for most SMBs  
- **Future expansion:** SMS digest variants  

### 2.3 Monthly Executive
- **Ideal customer:** Owners who want outcomes, not weekly homework  
- **Communication frequency:** Monthly summary + exceptions  
- **Automation level:** High (still gated)  
- **Approval behavior:** Pre-approved categories where policy allows; else batch monthly  
- **Exceptions:** Always interrupt for risk  
- **Recommended use:** After trust earned  
- **Future expansion:** Category-based auto-approve with caps  

### 2.4 Trusted Head of Marketing
- **Ideal customer:** High-trust, long tenure, stable brand  
- **Communication frequency:** Exception + monthly health  
- **Automation level:** Highest allowed by platform safety  
- **Approval behavior:** Interrupt only when needed  
- **Exceptions:** Broad and loud when fired  
- **Recommended use:** Explicit opt-in after promotion criteria  
- **Future expansion:** Tied to Executive Partner stage  

---

## 3. Interaction rules

1. **Style never silently disables safety.**  
2. **Stage and style are visible** in Settings/Account when those surfaces exist.  
3. **Promotion is celebrated** (magic moment) and **demotion is calm and explained**.  
4. **Admin can see trust progression**; customers see plain language, not internal scores.  
5. Until product builds style controls, **document the model** and keep current explicit approve → queue → publish behavior.
